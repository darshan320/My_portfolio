I'm a final-year Electronics & Communication Engineering student with a deep focus on building intelligence at the network edge — where microcontrollers meet machine learning and bandwidth is precious.

My work lives at the crossroads of three fields: embedded systems design, wireless network engineering, and applied ML research. I'm driven by a constraint-first philosophy — the best solutions squeeze maximum capability from minimum resources. That belief led me to TinyML, where I deploy compressed neural networks onto microcontrollers with kilobytes of RAM, and to network simulation, where I model real-world 5G and IoT scenarios before writing a single line of firmware.

Outside the lab, I actively participate in hackathons and IEEE student competitions, where rapid prototyping and cross-disciplinary thinking are rewarded. I'm also a curious reader of RF system design literature and an advocate for reproducible research practices in engineering education.
